## Design Context

### Users
Licensed architects, established studios, and multidisciplinary design teams screening public-sector opportunities that can realistically lead to built work, framework appointments, or downstream service contracts. They are usually scanning quickly, comparing many notices, and deciding where to spend pursuit time.

### Brand Personality
Severe, exacting, and editorial. The product should feel like a procurement desk for professionals rather than a glossy competition gallery. It should communicate confidence, calm scrutiny, and useful density.

### Aesthetic Direction
Brutally minimal, full-bleed, and information-forward. The browsing surface should feel like a procurement tear-sheet wall rebuilt as a restrained image-led grid: severe frames, tight typography, and minimal copy instead of glossy gallery theatrics. Avoid startup-dashboard glass, soft rounded tile nesting, and decorative gradients.

### Design Principles
1. The browse surface is the product. Default to immediate scanning, not landing-page storytelling.
2. Use typography, rhythm, thin rules, and strict card framing to organize complexity without turning the product into a lifestyle gallery.
3. Keep discover focused on title, location, deadline, value, and image at scan speed; push fuller evidence, qualification, and commercial trace into detail pages.
4. Filters must feel operational and fast, not like a settings sidebar.
5. Everything should bias toward professional procurement judgment, not inspiration-gallery aesthetics.
