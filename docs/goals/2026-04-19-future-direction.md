# Future Direction

## Goal

Build an AI-first procurement intelligence product for licensed architects and design teams screening public opportunities with a credible path to built work.

## Who It Serves

- Licensed architects
- Established studios
- Multidisciplinary design teams
- Practice managers and business development leads

## What To Do

- Focus on official-source-first opportunity discovery
- Expand cross-border procurement and design opportunity coverage
- Invest in normalization, evidence trace, and qualification scoring
- Help teams decide `bid / no-bid`, not just browse listings
- Build team workflow around watchlists, triage, notes, and next actions
- Offer verified official opportunity submission and review flows
- Add paid value around alerts, summaries, and pursuit workflow
- Explore official post-publication distribution services for authorities and organizers

## What Not To Do

- Do not become a generic competition gallery
- Do not become a homeowner renovation marketplace
- Do not allow free-form public posting directly into the public feed
- Do not weaken evidence rules for faster growth
- Do not optimize for vanity traffic over professional utility
- Do not drift into ad-led media without intelligence value
- Do not turn the product into a generic CRM or project-management tool

## Non-Negotiables

- Official facts outrank secondary discovery
- Missing facts remain unknown until verified
- The website consumes normalized records rather than inventing facts
- Procurement-first ranking matters more than visual inspiration
- New product copy and primary surfaces remain bilingual

## Winning Position

The product should not try to beat legacy players by looking newer.

It should win by being:

- faster at turning official notices into structured intelligence
- better at cross-country and multi-language normalization
- clearer about qualification fit and implementation path
- more useful for real team decisions after discovery

## Near-Term Direction

- Deepen the current intelligence product
- Charge studios for better filtering, alerting, and decision support
- Build operator-grade review flows before opening any external submission channel

## Explicit Non-Goals

- Consumer homeowner acquisition
- Quote comparison marketplace
- Ratings-and-reviews marketplace trust model
- Broad design inspiration or award showcase positioning
